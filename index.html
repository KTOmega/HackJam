What is HackJam?

For our 9th consecutive semester, there's a jam at Berkeley coming up. No, it's not a traffic jam. Welcome to HackJam: Talent. Prizes. Mentorship. Refreshments. All jammed into 4000 square feet of awesome.

At HackJam, hackers of all levels of experience get together for twelve hours of hacking and snacking. No pressure, we're all here to learn. We'll be running introductory workshops and 1:1 mentorship for those of you new to hacking; we'll have hardware and APIs for those who want to branch out into something new.

There will be friendship. There will be prizes. Come to HackJam - it's gonna be pretty sweet.

FAQ

Do I need to register or apply to attend HackJam?
Yes! Register here. Registration is non-competitive - we'll use this information for logistics and making the event ~hella baller~.

What if I show up without registering?
No food for you (because we'll have ordered food without knowing you'll be there). Seriously please register. It really helps us out :)

Do I need to be a Berkeley student?
Nope! Please be a student somewhere, though (both community college and high school are chill). If you're a non-student who wants to come, consider mentoring!

What if I'm in high school? Do I need to do anything special?
Nope, just come! We're not overnight so you don't need to fill out a form.

What if I'm inexperienced / have no idea what I'm doing?
We'll have mentorship, brainstorming workshops, and fellow-hacker meetups to help you get going. We're here for you. HackJam isn't competitive.
